## 1. Scope and Intent

### Objective
Define and lock the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) scope for the Trevor Mouse Tremor Filter project. This task establishes the boundaries of what will be included in the initial release, ensuring alignment with stakeholder expectations and technical feasibility.

### Primary Deliverables
- `SCOPE.md` document defining the MVP boundaries
- Approval from stakeholders on the scope
- Clear exclusion list for features not in MVP

### Success Criteria
- Scope document is comprehensive and unambiguous
- All stakeholders have reviewed and approved the scope
- Scope is frozen and serves as a reference for all downstream tasks

### Constraints
- Must align with the overall project vision and goals
- Must be feasible within the project timeline and resources
- Must not include features that are not essential for the MVP

### Assumptions
- Stakeholders are available for review and approval
- The scope will be used to finalize specifications and repository structure

### External Dependencies
- None

## 3. Functional Requirements

### 3.1 Scope Document Structure
The `SCOPE.md` document must include the following sections:
- **Introduction**: Overview of the project and MVP goals.
- **Included Features**: Detailed list of features included in the MVP.
- **Excluded Features**: Detailed list of features not included in the MVP.
- **Assumptions**: Key assumptions made during scope definition.
- **Constraints**: Constraints that influenced the scope.
- **Approval**: Section for stakeholder signatures and approval date.

### 3.2 Stakeholder Review Process
- The draft scope must be shared with all stakeholders for review.
- Feedback must be collected and incorporated into the scope document.
- Final approval must be obtained from all stakeholders before the scope is frozen.

---

## 4. Non-Functional Requirements

### 4.1 Quality
- The scope document must be clear, concise, and unambiguous.
- All terms and definitions must be consistent with the project glossary.

### 4.2 Compliance
- The scope must comply with the project's overall vision and goals.
- The scope must be feasible within the project timeline and resources.

### 4.3 Security
- The scope document must be stored securely and accessible only to authorized personnel.

---

## 5. User Interaction Flows

### 5.1 Stakeholder Review Flow
1. **Draft Creation**: The initial draft of the scope document is created.
2. **Review Request**: The draft is shared with stakeholders for review.
3. **Feedback Collection**: Feedback is collected from stakeholders.
4. **Incorporation**: Feedback is incorporated into the scope document.
5. **Final Review**: The updated scope document is reviewed by stakeholders.
6. **Approval**: Final approval is obtained from stakeholders.
7. **Freeze**: The scope is frozen and finalized.

---

## 6. Edge Cases and Failure Scenarios

### 6.1 Stakeholder Disagreement
- **Scenario**: Stakeholders disagree on the scope.
- **Mitigation**: Facilitate a discussion to resolve disagreements and reach a consensus.

### 6.2 Feedback Overload
- **Scenario**: Too much feedback is received, making it difficult to incorporate all suggestions.
- **Mitigation**: Prioritize feedback based on its impact on the project goals and feasibility.

### 6.3 Scope Creep
- **Scenario**: Stakeholders request additional features that are not essential for the MVP.
- **Mitigation**: Clearly communicate the importance of staying within the MVP boundaries and defer non-essential features to future releases.
