## 3. Functional Requirements

### 3.1 Specifications Bundle
The specifications bundle must include the following:
- **Technical Specifications**: Detailed technical specifications for all components.
- **API Specifications**: API specifications for all interfaces.
- **UI Specifications**: UI specifications for all user interfaces.
- **Performance Specifications**: Performance specifications for all components.

### 3.2 Stakeholder Review Process
- The draft specifications must be shared with all stakeholders for review.
- Feedback must be collected and incorporated into the specifications.
- Final approval must be obtained from all stakeholders before the specifications are finalized.

## 4. Non-Functional Requirements

### 4.1 Quality
- The specifications must be clear, concise, and unambiguous.

### 4.2 Compliance
- The specifications must comply with the project's overall vision and goals.
- The specifications must be feasible within the project timeline and resources.

### 4.3 Security
- The specifications must be stored securely and accessible only to authorized personnel.

## 5. User Interaction Flows

### 5.1 Stakeholder Review Flow
1. **Draft Creation**: The initial draft of the specifications is created.
2. **Review Request**: The draft is shared with stakeholders for review.
3. **Feedback Collection**: Feedback is collected from stakeholders.
4. **Incorporation**: Feedback is incorporated into the specifications.
5. **Final Review**: The updated specifications are reviewed by stakeholders.
6. **Approval**: Final approval is obtained from stakeholders.
7. **Finalization**: The specifications are finalized.

---

## 6. Edge Cases and Failure Scenarios

### 6.1 Stakeholder Disagreement
- **Scenario**: Stakeholders disagree on the specifications.
- **Mitigation**: Facilitate a discussion to resolve disagreements and reach a consensus.

### 6.2 Feedback Overload
- **Scenario**: Too much feedback is received, making it difficult to incorporate all suggestions.
- **Mitigation**: Prioritize feedback based on its impact on the project goals and feasibility.

### 6.3 Scope Creep
- **Scenario**: Stakeholders request additional specifications that are not essential for the MVP.
- **Mitigation**: Clearly communicate the importance of staying within the MVP boundaries and defer non-essential specifications to future releases.
